# Page build checklist — LBM Solutions (blockchain & AI agency)

A living tracker. Tick `[x]` when a page is built, reviewed, and live.

## How this is organized — three tiers

The menu and the ad footprint are different layers. Don't confuse "few items in the nav" with "few pages."

- **Tier 1 — Core site:** indexed, in the nav/footer. Kept lean on purpose (positioning + trust). This is your locked set.
- **Tier 2 — Organic service sub-pages:** the full universe of services a pro blockchain agency offers. These live under hub pages or in the mega-menu's deeper level, NOT the top nav. Add them as real demand + provable case studies appear.
- **Tier 3 — Paid ad landing pages:** noindex, no nav, one CTA. As many as you have credible campaigns for. Built and tracked separately from the site.

Each Tier 2 / Tier 3 line carries a gate reminder. Before building, confirm: **(D)** real search demand, **(P)** provable case study / proof on the page, **(I)** ICP-fit leads (funded fintech / SaaS / protocol founders).

- [ ] LP: stablecoin development / issuance — (D) med (P) issuance proof (I) fintech
- [ ] LP: crypto wallet development — (D) high (P) gold-standard page already (I) mixed
- [ ] LP: crypto exchange development — (D) high (P) exchange built (I) mixed
- [ ] LP: DeFi protocol development — (D) high (P) named protocol (I) protocols
- [ ] LP: AI agent development — (D) med-high (P) agent shipped + ROI (I) SaaS/fintech

**Gate rule:** if (P) fails — you can't prove it on the page — do NOT bid on the narrow term. Fold it into a broader page you can back, and earn the case study first. A clicked ad that lands on an unprovable claim funds the visitor's skepticism; that's worse than not bidding.

Per ad landing page, each must have:
- [ ] noindex, nofollow
- [ ] canonical → matching main service page
- [ ] no main site navigation
- [ ] one conversion goal / one primary CTA (outcome-named, e.g. "Get my audit quote")
- [ ] message match: ad's key phrase repeated in the H1
- [ ] shortest viable form + trust elements beside it
- [ ] mobile: 3 questions (where am I / what can I do / why) answered in first viewport; sticky CTA
- [ ] compact footer (legal only — see below)
- [ ] GA4 + Ads conversion events + UTM capture
- [ ] LCP < 1.8s on 4G

## Ad landing page — linking rules (your footer question)

**From the main site → the ad page:** not linked at all. The ad page is deliberately orphaned and set to noindex. It is reached only by clicking the paid ad. It does not appear in the nav, the sitewide footer, the sitemap, or any internal link. This protects your main pages' SEO authority (no duplicate-content cannibalization) and keeps the paid experience clean.

**On the ad page itself → outbound links:** almost none, by design. The principle is a 1:1 attention ratio — one page, one action. The page has NO top nav and NO full sitewide footer with all your service links. The only links allowed are the legal essentials: privacy policy and terms (Google Ads policy effectively requires a reachable privacy policy). A compact footer carrying just those legal links plus a credibility line (office locations, contact email) is the accepted version. Everything else — service menu, blog, related pages, social icons — is removed, because every extra link is an exit path that lowers conversion.

So: standalone, yes — but not a naked page. It keeps a minimal legal footer and one CTA, and it is not wired into the rest of the site in either direction.

1. Tier 1 foundation: home, about, contact, pricing, solutions hub.
2. Tier 1 flagship wedges: smart-contract-audits, stablecoin-payment-rails, ai-agents-and-automation, tokenization-and-rwa.

4. Tier 3 paid pages for the 3–4 flagship wedges (where proof already exists).
